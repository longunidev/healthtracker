Tests for Diagnosing Hypertension

  • Blood Pressure Test

    Measuring blood pressure is a fundamental step in determining the presence of hypertension.

  • Primary Care Monitoring

    Regular blood pressure measurements at your primary care physician's office help assess hypertension.

  • 24-Hour Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring

    Continuous blood pressure monitoring over a day aids in understanding hypertension patterns.

  • Blood Tests

    Examining kidney function, blood sugar, cholesterol, and other markers provides insights into hypertension risks.

  • Urine Tests

    Analyzing urine for protein, white blood cells, and other indicators assists in hypertension diagnosis.

  • Electrocardiogram (ECG) Test

    An ECG helps detect heart diseases associated with hypertension, offering a comprehensive diagnosis.

  • Ultrasound Test

    Ultrasound imaging is valuable for assessing organ damage caused by hypertension, such as the kidneys or heart.

  • CT or MRI Test

    Advanced imaging techniques like CT or MRI aid in identifying organ damage, including potential brain hemorrhage.

  • Additional Information

    Early detection through these tests is crucial for effective hypertension management. Regular health check-ups and consultations with healthcare professionals contribute to overall cardiovascular well-being.
