Understanding and Diagnosing Hypotension

  • Blood Test for Comprehensive Insight

    If you suspect chronic hypotension, comprehensive blood tests are crucial. These tests measure blood sugar levels and red blood cell count to provide insights into your blood health and identify signs of hypotension.

  • Tilt Table Test for Orthostatic Hypotension

    A tilt table test is a prevalent method for diagnosing orthostatic hypotension. During the test, your heart rate, blood pressure, and symptoms are monitored as the table is gradually tilted upright. A drop of 20mmHg in systolic or 10mmHg in diastolic below the baseline indicates orthostatic hypotension.

    If symptoms occur during the test, immediate return to a supine position is necessary.

  • Electrocardiogram (ECG) for Heart Screening

    To screen for heart issues causing blood pressure drops, an electrocardiogram (ECG) is essential. This non-invasive procedure uses electrodes on your chest, arms, and legs to detect the heart's electrical signals. ECG can identify irregular heart rhythms, structural issues, and other cardiac conditions.

  • Additional Considerations

    For a more comprehensive assessment, your healthcare provider may combine these tests with a physical examination, medical history review, and ambulatory monitoring to capture blood pressure variations throughout the day.
