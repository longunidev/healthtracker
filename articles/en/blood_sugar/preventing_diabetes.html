Preventing Diabetes

  • Type 1 Diabetes and Prevention

    Type 1 diabetes is inherently unavoidable. However, there's encouraging news for individuals with prediabetes, type 2 diabetes, and gestational diabetes. Here are actionable steps to delay or prevent these conditions.

  • Healthy Food Choices

    Opt for foods lower in calories and fats while rich in fiber to effectively manage blood sugar levels. This approach reduces daily calorie intake and aids in weight loss. Include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and seafood in your diet, and opt for water over sugary beverages. Avoid saturated fats like butter, animal fat, coconut oil, or palm oil. Consume small, balanced meals throughout the day.

  • Regular Exercise

    Initiate a gradual habit of engaging in at least 30 minutes of physical activity five days a week. Opt for moderate aerobic exercises such as biking, brisk walking, hiking, and more to promote overall well-being.

  • Weight Management

    Use a BMI Calculator to determine your weight range. If overweight, shedding 7% of your body weight can significantly impact diabetes prevention. For individuals with gestational diabetes, consult a healthcare professional for guidance on maintaining a healthy weight during pregnancy.

  • Smoking Cessation

    Smoking is linked to an increased risk of diabetes development. To prevent diabetes, steer clear of tobacco products and embrace a smoke-free lifestyle.

  • Blood Pressure Control

    Individuals with high blood pressure face a higher risk of developing type 2 diabetes. Hence, normal blood pressure is crucial in preventing diabetes, complementing the control of blood sugar levels.

    Adopting a healthy lifestyle—comprising nutritious eating, regular exercise, maintaining a healthy weight, moderate alcohol consumption, and limited salt intake—contributes to effective blood pressure control. This holistic approach not only safeguards against high blood pressure but also helps manage blood sugar levels.

  • Additional Insights

    Ensuring a holistic approach to health can lead to better outcomes. Beyond preventing diabetes, embracing a healthy lifestyle positively influences various aspects of well-being. Regular health check-ups and consultations with healthcare professionals contribute to overall health management.
