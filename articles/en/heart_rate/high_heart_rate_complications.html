High Heart Rate Complications

  • Introduction

    Usually, your heart rate can occasionally exceed the normal range, which is normal when you are exercising, under stress, or have a related disease.

  • Signs of Concern

    If your resting heart rate rises over 100 beats per minute not because of psychological stress, it may be a sign of a serious heart condition, tachycardia. Tachycardia can increase the risk of stroke, heart failure, sudden cardiac arrest, and even death.

  • Study Findings

    The Copenhagen Male Study in 1971, monitoring 3,000 middle-aged men's health and resting heart rate for 16 years, discovered that every 10 to 22 increase in resting heart rate per minute raised the risk of death by 16%. For instance, people who had a resting heart rate over 90 beats per minute elevated their risk of death by 120% to 150%.

  • Complications of Tachycardia

    • Fainting
    • Dizziness
    • Shortness of breath
    • Fatigue
    • Angina
    • Heart palpitations
    • Heart failure
    • Stroke
    • Blood clots
    • Cardiac arrest
    • Sudden death

    If you experience extreme pain or discomfort with your palpitations, seek emergency help immediately.
