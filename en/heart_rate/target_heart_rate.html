Target Heart Rate

  • Introduction

    Target heart rate (THR or training heart rate) is the suggested range of how often your heart beats during exercise for improving health. It is an important index for you to exercise safely.

  • Calculating Maximum Heart Rate

    Your THR depends on the highest number of times your heart can beat in one minute, which is your maximum heart rate. For men, the estimated maximum heart rate can be calculated as 220 minus their age. For women, the Northwestern Medicine research suggests using the equation HRmax = 206 - (0.88 x your age) to get an accurate result.

  • Target Heart Rate Zones

    If you are doing moderate-intensity activity, your THR should be between 50% to 70% of your maximum heart rate. For vigorous exercise, your THR should be between 70% to 85% of your maximum heart rate.

    For example, a 40-year-old man's maximum heart rate is 180 (220 - 40). The THR should be 50% to 85% of 180, in other words, 90 to 153 beats per minute.
