Resting Heart Rate

  • Introduction

    If you wish to know your current or future heart health condition, resting heart rate (RHR) is an important indicator you must know.

  • Importance of RHR

    As everyone knows, the heart pumps blood and oxygen throughout the body to keep it functioning well. The RHR is the number of heart beats per minute while you're at rest, showing whether your heart muscle is functioning well or not.

    People with healthy hearts usually have RHR between 60 and 100 beats per minute. Irregularities, or values lower than 60 or higher than 100 per minute, may indicate potential heart problems that should be brought to the attention of you and your doctor.

  • Measuring RHR

    To measure your RHR, find a time when you are at rest, put your index and middle finger on the thumb side of your wrist or on either side of your neck to feel the pulse. Count how many times it beats in 30 seconds and double the number to calculate your RHR per minute.

    An alternative is to use our app, which only requires showing one of your fingertips under your mobile camera to calculate your RHR.

  • Ensuring Accuracy

    To ensure accuracy and identify irregularities, it is recommended to measure your RHR multiple times throughout the day or week and calculate an average number.

    Your RHR may change throughout the day due to environmental factors, activity levels, measurement timing, etc.

  • Best Practices

    For the most accurate results, the American Heart Association recommends checking your RHR in the morning after a night's sleep and before getting out of bed. Additionally, wait for at least one to two hours after consuming caffeine, exercising, or experiencing a stressful situation.
