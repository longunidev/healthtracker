Understanding Heart Rate

Your heart rate, also known as pulse, represents the number of heartbeats per minute.

With each heartbeat, the heart pumps blood throughout the body, supplying oxygen for proper bodily functions. The heart's workload in circulating blood varies based on different states, such as rest or exercise.

Two key heart rate indexes are the resting heart rate and target heart rate. The resting heart rate measures how your heart beats when you are at rest, while the target heart rate indicates the ideal rate during exercise.

Resting Heart Rate Ranges
Age Group Resting Heart Rate Range (beats per minute)
Children (1-2 years) 80-130
Children (3-4 years) 80-120
Children (5-6 years) 75-115
Children (7-9 years) 70-110
Children (10-15 years) 70-100
People (over 15 years old) 60-100

During exercise, training intensity can be gauged using target heart rate ranges:

  • Warm Up: Less than 60% of HRmax
  • Target Training: 60% to 79% of HRmax
  • High-intensity: 80% to 89% of HRmax
  • Extreme: Equal to or greater than 90% of HRmax

Please be aware that our app is not a substitute for professional healthcare services. If you experience consistently elevated heart rate or any heart-related concerns, it is crucial to consult your doctor promptly.
