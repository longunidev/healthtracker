Tests for Diagnosing Hypertension

  • Blood Pressure Test

    Measuring blood pressure to determine if hypertension exists.

  • Primary Care Monitoring

    Multiple blood pressure measurements taken at a primary care physician's office to determine if hypertension exists.

  • 24-Hour Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring

    Multiple blood pressure measurements taken within a day to determine if hypertension exists.

  • Blood Tests

    Checking kidney function, blood sugar, cholesterol, and other indicators to determine if hypertension exists.

  • Urine Tests

    Checking for protein, white blood cells, and other indicators in urine to determine if hypertension exists.

  • Electrocardiogram (ECG) Test

    Checking for heart disease caused by hypertension through an ECG.

  • Ultrasound Test

    Checking for organ damage caused by hypertension, such as the kidneys or heart, through an ultrasound.

  • CT or MRI Test

    Checking for organ damage caused by hypertension, such as brain hemorrhage, through imaging.

  • Additional Information

    Early detection of hypertension is crucial for effective management. Regular health check-ups and consultation with healthcare professionals can provide valuable insights into your cardiovascular health.
