Recognizing Symptoms of Hypertension

  • Severe Headache

    If hypertension leads to increased pressure in brain blood vessels, it may result in severe headaches, indicating a medical emergency. Damaged blood vessels in the brain can lead to throbbing headaches worsening with slight activity.

  • Dizziness

    Dizziness should not be dismissed, as persistent high blood pressure is a major contributor to stroke. Sudden dizziness, difficulty maintaining balance, and an unsteady gait may indicate an imminent stroke.

  • Nausea

    Episodes of sudden nausea and loss of appetite in individuals with hypertension may signify a hypertensive crisis. Nausea, often accompanied by dizziness, can indicate extremely high blood pressure.

  • Difficulty of Breath

    Elevated blood pressure can impact heart and lung function, resulting in symptoms such as shortness of breath, especially during physical activity or exercise.

  • Chest Pain

    Hypertension is a significant risk factor for heart attack and heart failure. Arteries hardening due to high blood pressure can lead to reduced blood supply to the heart, causing chest pain (angina).

  • Blurred Vision or Other Vision Changes

    Extremely high blood pressure can damage blood vessels in the eyes, causing symptoms like blurred vision or, in severe cases, vision loss.

  • Nosebleeds

    While nosebleeds are not typical for hypertension, extreme blood pressure levels can lead to vessel damage in the nose, resulting in bleeding. Immediate medical attention is necessary in such cases.

  • Additional Information

    Regular monitoring of blood pressure, a balanced diet, and a healthy lifestyle are essential for managing hypertension and preventing associated complications.
