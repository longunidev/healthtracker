Guidelines for Blood Pressure Management Throughout Pregnancy

  • Before Pregnancy

    Before conceiving, it is essential to develop a pregnancy strategy. Consult with your healthcare professional about any health issues or medications you are taking. They can guide you on safe medications during pregnancy and provide advice on maintaining a healthy weight through proper nutrition and regular physical activity.

  • During Pregnancy

    Initiate prenatal care promptly and attend all scheduled appointments. Communicate with your healthcare provider about your medications and their safety during pregnancy. Monitor blood pressure at home using a personal device, promptly notifying your healthcare provider of any concerns. Focus on maintaining a healthy diet and weight to support a successful pregnancy.

  • After Pregnancy

    Post-pregnancy, refrain from smoking, which elevates blood pressure and increases the risk of heart-related issues. If you smoke, reducing or quitting will significantly lower your risk for heart disease. Prioritize a smoke-free environment for both your well-being and that of your newborn.

    Additionally, continue the practices learned during pregnancy, such as maintaining a healthy lifestyle, to promote long-term cardiovascular health.

  • Additional Tips

    1. Engage in low-impact exercises suitable for pregnant women to promote physical well-being.

    2. Include a variety of nutrient-rich foods in your diet to ensure optimal health for both mother and baby.

    3. Stay hydrated by consuming an adequate amount of water throughout the day.
