Managing Hypertension: Medications and Dietary Tips

  • Common Drugs Harmful for Hypertension

    Learn about drugs that may negatively impact hypertension and why caution is advised:

  • Nonsteroidal Anti-inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs)

    Examples include ibuprofen and naproxen, which can raise blood pressure and affect hypertension medications.

  • Decongestants

    Often found in cold and allergy medications, decongestants can narrow blood vessels, leading to increased blood pressure.

  • Corticosteroids

    Used to treat inflammation, these drugs may cause sodium retention and fluid buildup, contributing to higher blood pressure.

  • Hormonal Contraceptives

    Birth control pills and similar contraceptives can raise blood pressure in certain women.

  • Stimulants

    Drugs like amphetamines and cocaine can lead to a sudden spike in blood pressure, increasing the risk of stroke and heart attack.

  • Cautionary Note

    While not everyone with hypertension reacts the same way, limiting the use of these medications is generally recommended. Consult your doctor for alternative treatments and personalized advice.
