The Impact of Elevated Blood Pressure

  • Cardiovascular Complications

    Elevated blood pressure can harm the arteries supplying blood to the heart, contributing to conditions such as coronary artery disease, heart attacks, and heart failure.

  • Brain Health Risks

    Elevated blood pressure can inflict damage on blood vessels in the brain, increasing the likelihood of a stroke.

  • Peripheral Artery Concerns

    Elevated blood pressure can negatively impact arteries supplying blood to the legs and feet, leading to peripheral artery disease. This can result in discomfort, numbness, and weakness in the lower limbs.

  • Kidney Function Impairment

    Elevated blood pressure can impair the blood vessels in the kidneys, diminishing their ability to function optimally.

  • Cognitive Decline and Dementia

    Elevated blood pressure heightens the risk of cognitive decline and dementia, particularly in older adults.
