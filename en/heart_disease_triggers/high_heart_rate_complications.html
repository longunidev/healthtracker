High Heart Rate Complications

  • Your heart rate may occasionally exceed the normal range, especially during exercise, stress, or due to a related disease.

  • However, if your resting heart rate consistently surpasses 100 beats per minute without psychological stress, it could indicate a serious heart condition known as tachycardia. This condition increases the risk of stroke, heart failure, sudden cardiac arrest, and even death.

    A study conducted by the Copenhagen Male Study in 1971, monitoring the health and resting heart rate of 3,000 middle-aged men for 16 years, revealed that every 10 to 22 increase in resting heart rate per minute raised the risk of death by 16%. For example, individuals with a resting heart rate over 90 beats per minute elevated their risk of death by 120% to 150%.

    In addition to the heightened risk of death, a high resting heart rate can lead to various complications. While some individuals with tachycardia may not experience symptoms, others may encounter complications triggered by a rapid heartbeat.

  • Complications of tachycardia:

    • Fainting
    • Dizziness
    • Shortness of breath
    • Fatigue
    • Angina
    • Heart palpitations
    • Heart failure
    • Stroke
    • Blood clots
    • Cardiac arrest
    • Sudden death

    If you experience extreme pain or discomfort with your palpitations, seek emergency help immediately.
