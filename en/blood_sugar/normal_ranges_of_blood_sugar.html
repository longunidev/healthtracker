Normal Ranges of Blood Sugar

  • Understanding Blood Sugar Levels

    Knowledge of normal blood sugar ranges is crucial for individuals managing their blood sugar levels. These levels can vary before eating, after fasting, and post-meal. It's noteworthy that children exhibit different blood sugar ranges compared to adults.

  • Normal Ranges for Adults

    • Before meals or fasting: 4.0 to 5.4 mmol/L (72 to 99 mg/dL)
    • At least 90 minutes after eating: Under 7.8 mmol/L (140 mg/dL)

  • Diabetic Ranges for Adults

    Adults with diabetes have specific blood sugar targets, varying for type 1 and type 2 diabetes. These targets include levels upon waking, before meals or fasting, and post-meal.

    • Type 1 diabetes:
      • Upon waking: 5.0 to 7.0 mmol/L (90 to 126 mg/dL)
      • Before meals or fasting: 4.0 to 7.0 mmol/L (72 to 126 mg/dL)
      • At least 90 minutes after eating: 5.0 to 9.0 mmol/L (90 to 162 mg/dL)
    • Type 2 diabetes:
      • Before meals or when fasting: 4.0 and 7.0 mmol/L (72 to 126 mg/dL)
      • At least 90 minutes after eating: under 8.5 mmol/L (153 mg/dL)

  • Children Blood Sugar Ranges

    Children's blood sugar levels vary depending on age. Here are the normal ranges:

    • Under 6: 4.5 to 11.1 mmol/L (80 to 200 mg/dL) each day
    • Between 6 and 12:
      • When fasting: 4.5 to 10 mmol/L (80 to 180 mg/dL)
      • Before meals: 5 to 10 mmol/L (90 to 180 mg/dL)
      • One to two hours after meals: under 7.8 mmol/L (140 mg/dL)
    • Ages 13 to 19:
      • When fasting: between 3.9 and 8.3 mmol/L (70 to 150 mg/dL)
      • Before meals: from 5 to 7.2 mmol/L (90 to 130 mg/dL)
      • One to two hours after meals: under 7.8 mmol/L (140 mg/dL)

  • Hypoglycemia

    Aside from normal and diabetic blood sugar ranges, individuals may experience hypoglycemia when levels drop below 3.89 mmol/L (70 mg/dL). Symptoms may not appear until levels drop under 3 mmol/L (55 mg/dL).
